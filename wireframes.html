Pakages Page WireFrames

Wireframes for Pakages Page

Hero Section

Similar to the to all the other page, I chose a prominent heading here that clearly indicates the page’s focus: showcasing our packages. The navigation in the top-right corner remains consistent, reinforcing a stable, recognizable layout that enhances ease of use. This consistency across pages is critical for users, as it promotes familiarity and reduces cognitive load.

Video Section

The video section here serves as a dynamic introduction to the package options. By showing a video before presenting detailed package descriptions, I’m giving users an opportunity to experience our style and storytelling approach. The video acts as a teaser, drawing users in with visual examples of what they can expect from each package.

Clickable Package & Description

The clickable images for each package are carefully placed to offer users a straightforward way to explore specific services in detail. These images serve as direct entry points, allowing users to explore packages that resonate with them visually. By making them clickable, I am removing extra steps in the navigation process.

Each package description is positioned next to the image gallery to provide users with further detail. This hierarchy—from visuals to text—was chosen to engage users initially through imagery, then provide supporting information for those who want a deeper understanding of our offerings.

Our Work Wireframes

Wireframes for Our Work Page

Hero Section:

At the top of the page, I’ve implemented a hero section that shows the heading “OUR WORK” with the studio name “RED POCKET STUDIO.” The large, centered typography and minimal design here create a powerful first impression. This choice is meant to establish professionalism and give users a sense of trust in the studio’s capabilities. By using a simple, direct heading,I guide users to immediately understand the purpose of the page without overwhelming them with too much information.

Bouncing Icon for Scrolling

Directly under the hero section, there’s a subtle bouncing icon. I included this as a visual cue to improve engagement, ensuring users don’t miss the content below (suggested by Imaan).

Picture Cards - Click & Flip Interaction

Directly beneath the hero section, I’ve implemented a clickable, flip-style image gallery. Each picture card is designed to flip when clicked, creating an interactive experience that invites users to engage with the content actively rather than passively. The flip animation provides visual feedback and offers a sense of discovery, encouraging users to explore each element in a playful, yet purposeful way. This approach also breaks away from a static image gallery, adding dynamism to the layout.

Video Section

The video segment is situated towards the bottom of the page, offering an immersive, multimedia experience. By placing this content at the end, I create a hierarchy where images lead to text, which then transitions into video. This progression reflects the user journey — capturing attention with visuals, building trust with descriptive text, and then fully engaging users with motion and sound.

Design Page Wireframes

Wireframes for Design Page

clickable Side Menu

The side menu provides direct access to various design categories, which allows users to navigate specific sections based on their interests. Placing it on the left side gives users a consistent frame of reference as they scroll, making it easier to locate and switch between sections.

Content Section

Rather than displaying all information at once, I’ve incorporated "Read More" buttons within each section, allowing users to engage with additional content if they’re interested. The collapsible design is especially useful for mobile and smaller screens, where excessive text can feel overwhelming. The "Read More" buttons therefore balance accessibility with a clean, organized layout, allowing users to control their own information intake.
